# Appendix C  
## Beyond the Basics: Practical Libraries for Data Analysts

Many analysts develop a small set of favorite tools that simplify common analytical tasks.  
Below are several libraries widely used in clinical, research, and data science workflows for exploratory analysis, reporting, visualization, and data quality assessment.

---

### TableOne (R and Python)
Creates standardized **Table 1 summaries** commonly used in biomedical research manuscripts. These tables summarize demographic and baseline characteristics across study cohorts and are widely used in clinical publications.

R: https://cran.r-project.org/package=tableone  
Python: https://pypi.org/project/tableone/

---

### Sweetviz (Python)
Automated exploratory data analysis reports that highlight distributions, correlations, and potential data quality issues. Generates a visually rich HTML report summarizing dataset structure and variable relationships.

https://github.com/fbdesignpro/sweetviz

---

### YData-Profiling (formerly pandas-profiling)
Creates comprehensive exploratory data analysis reports with minimal code. The tool generates interactive HTML summaries including missing values, correlations, distributions, and alerts about potential data issues.

https://ydata-profiling.ydata.ai

---

### HADES (OHDSI R Libraries)
A suite of R packages supporting large-scale observational health data analysis within the **OHDSI ecosystem**. The framework enables reproducible analytics across standardized healthcare datasets such as OMOP CDM.

https://ohdsi.org/software-tools/
