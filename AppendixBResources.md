# Literature and Research Discovery

### PubMed  
**[PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov)**  

A primary database for biomedical literature maintained by the U.S. National Library of Medicine. PubMed indexes millions of citations across biomedical and life sciences journals and is typically the starting point for systematic literature searches.

---

### ClinicalTrials.gov  
**[ClinicalTrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov)**  

A global registry of clinical studies maintained by the U.S. National Institutes of Health. The database allows researchers to identify ongoing, completed, and unpublished trials and provides structured information about study design and outcomes.

---

### JANE – Journal Author Name Estimator  
**[JANE](https://jane.biosemantics.org)**  

A free tool that suggests relevant journals and potential peer reviewers by comparing a manuscript title or abstract with articles indexed in PubMed.

**Citation**

Schuemie MJ, Kors JA, Mons B.  
*JANE: suggesting journals, finding experts.*  
Bioinformatics. 2008.

---

### UpToDate  
**[UpToDate](https://www.wolterskluwer.com/en/solutions/uptodate)**  

A widely used clinical decision-support platform that summarizes evidence on diseases, diagnostics, and treatments. Analysts frequently consult it to understand clinical context when interpreting health data.

# Drug Repurposing and Therapeutic Discovery

### CURE ID  
**[CURE ID](https://cure.ncats.io/home)**  

CURE ID is a free platform developed by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and the National Institutes of Health (NIH) to collect and share reports of novel uses of existing drugs, including off-label treatments and drug repurposing efforts.

The platform allows clinicians and researchers to explore real-world treatment experiences for difficult-to-treat conditions and identify potential therapeutic opportunities that may not yet be reflected in formal clinical trials.

---

### Every Cure  
**[Every Cure](https://everycure.org)**  

A nonprofit initiative focused on identifying overlooked therapeutic uses for existing drugs. By combining biomedical literature, clinical data, and computational methods, the organization aims to accelerate drug repurposing for diseases with limited treatment options.

# Reproducibility and Data Management

### FAIR Data Principles  
**[FAIR Principles](https://www.go-fair.org/fair-principles)**  

Guidelines for improving scientific data stewardship by ensuring that research data are **Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable (FAIR).**

**Citation**

Wilkinson MD, Dumontier M, Aalbersberg IJ, et al.  
*The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship.*  
Scientific Data. 2016.  
https://doi.org/10.1038/sdata.2016.18

---

### DMPTool  
**[DMPTool](https://dmptool.org)**  

An online platform that helps researchers create formal data management plans required by many funding agencies. It supports best practices for documenting, sharing, and preserving research data.

---

### EQUATOR Network  
**[EQUATOR Network](https://www.equator-network.org)**  

An international initiative promoting transparent reporting of health research. The site hosts reporting guidelines such as CONSORT, STROBE, and PRISMA that improve reproducibility and clarity in scientific publications.

**Citation**

Simera I, Moher D, Hoey J, Schulz KF, Altman DG.  
*Transparent and accurate reporting increases reliability, utility, and impact of research.*  
PLoS Medicine. 2010.

---

### GitHub Quickstart Guide  
**[GitHub Hello World Guide](https://docs.github.com/en/get-started/quickstart/hello-world)**  

An introductory tutorial explaining how to use GitHub for version control and collaborative software development. GitHub is widely used in scientific research to share code, track changes, and support reproducible workflows.

---

### Improving Reproducibility of Data Analysis and Code in Medical Research  

**[BMJ Open Article](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12496075)**  

Streiber AM, Hoepel SJW, Blok E, et al.  
*Improving reproducibility of data analysis and code in medical research: five recommendations to get started.*  
BMJ Open. 2025.

### The Art of Data Science  
**[The Art of Data Science](https://leanpub.com/artofdatascience)**

# OMOP and Data Harmonization

### OHDSI Community  
**[OHDSI](https://ohdsi.org)**  

An international open-science collaborative developing methods, software, and standards for large-scale observational health data analysis.

**Citation**

Hripcsak G, Duke JD, Shah NH, et al.  
*Observational Health Data Sciences and Informatics (OHDSI): opportunities for observational researchers.*  
Studies in Health Technology and Informatics. 2015.

---

### OHDSI Athena  
**[OHDSI Athena](https://athena.ohdsi.org)**  

Athena is the vocabulary distribution platform maintained by the OHDSI community. It provides curated downloads of standardized vocabularies used within the OMOP Common Data Model, including mappings for SNOMED CT, LOINC, RxNorm, and ICD.

Researchers use Athena to obtain the vocabulary tables required to build and maintain OMOP databases and ensure consistent terminology mapping across clinical datasets.

---

### The Book of OHDSI  
**[The Book of OHDSI](https://ohdsi.github.io/TheBookOfOhdsi)**  

An open textbook describing the OHDSI ecosystem including the OMOP Common Data Model and the analytic tools used in observational research.

---

### OMOP Common Data Model Documentation  
**[OMOP CDM Documentation](https://ohdsi.github.io/CommonDataModel/index.html)**  

Technical documentation describing the OMOP Common Data Model, which standardizes clinical datasets to support large-scale observational research.

---

### MIMIC-IV OMOP Demo Dataset  
**[MIMIC-IV OMOP Demo](https://physionet.org/content/mimic-iv-demo-omop)**  

A publicly available dataset derived from the MIMIC clinical database and converted into OMOP format. It is widely used for training and testing observational research pipelines.

**Citation**

Johnson AEW, Pollard TJ, Shen L, et al.  
*MIMIC-IV: a freely accessible electronic health record dataset.*  
Scientific Data. 2023.

# Responsible Data Science and Bias

### Dissecting Racial Bias in an Algorithm Used to Manage the Health of Populations  

**[Science Article](https://science.sciencemag.org/content/366/6464/447)**  

Obermeyer Z, Powers B, Vogeli C, Mullainathan S.  
*Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm used to manage the health of populations.*  
Science. 2019.

This influential study demonstrated how clinical algorithms can encode systemic bias when cost is used as a proxy for health need, highlighting the importance of fairness and transparency in health data science.
